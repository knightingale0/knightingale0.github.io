In our first meeting we discussed some general ideas for the cipher, and the main one that stuck was the idea of splitting the message into chunks. Initially the plan was to have three, regardless of message length or keys, and then apply some simpler cipher method to each chunk and recombine the encrypted text to get our full ciphertext. This concept made it to the final design, though with the caveat that the number is based on a key, rather than a set value. Other thoughts from the initial meeting included having a first phase of encryption where we would encrypt the key we would use on the text, to hopefully throw off decryption, as well as the idea of swapping letters. These were cut because it added another key without adding another layer of encryption to the actual text, and took a long time to encrypt and decrypt, which did add security but pushed us close to the 15 minute limit.
